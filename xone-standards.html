Our Commitment to NIST Standards

xOne Secure’s cybersecurity services are built on the robust foundation of NIST standards, which are mandated for federal agencies and contractors handling sensitive data. We align with two cornerstone frameworks: NIST SP 800-171 Revision 3 and NIST CSF 2.0, ensuring compliance with DoD requirements and delivering best-in-class security for all clients.

[](https://www.nist.gov/publications)

NIST SP 800-171 Revision 3, finalized in May 2024, provides 97 controls to protect CUI in non-federal systems, critical for DoD contractors handling intellectual property or weapons system data. We implement controls like access management, incident response, and supply chain risk management (newly added in Revision 3) to safeguard CUI. NIST’s machine-readable formats (JSON, Excel) allow us to streamline compliance, making audits transparent and efficient. Our team ensures your systems meet these standards, reducing the risk of breaches that could jeopardize DoD contracts.

[](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2024/05/nist-finalizes-updated-guidelines-protecting-sensitive-information)

NIST CSF 2.0, updated in February 2024, offers a flexible framework with five core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. Mandated by Executive Order 13800, it’s the gold standard for cybersecurity risk management across industries. We use CSF 2.0 to map your assets, assess risks, and develop proactive strategies, ensuring your organization can detect threats early and recover swiftly. Think of it as a cybersecurity GPS—our experts are the cartographers, navigating you through the threat landscape with NIST as our guide.

[](https://www.nist.gov/publications)

By adhering to DFARS clause 252.204-7012 and CMMC requirements, we go beyond compliance to deliver resilience. Our small, elite team doesn’t just check boxes; we craft solutions that anticipate threats, ensuring your systems are a fortress against adversaries like nation-state actors. Whether you’re bidding for DoD contracts or securing commercial networks, our NIST-driven approach builds trust and delivers results.

How We Apply NIST Standards to Our Services

Our core services—Network Infrastructure Assessment, Auditing & Compliance, and Penetration Testing—are NIST-powered missions designed to protect and empower our clients. Here’s how we weave NIST standards into each service to ensure DoD-grade security.

Network Infrastructure Assessment
NIST Alignment: We leverage NIST SP 800-171’s risk assessment and system monitoring controls (e.g., 03.11.01 Risk Assessment) to evaluate your network architecture, cloud systems, and endpoints. CSF 2.0’s Identify and Protect functions guide our analysis of assets and vulnerabilities.
[](https://www.nist.gov/publications) Process: Our team conducts vulnerability scanning, traffic analysis, and configuration reviews to ensure CUI protection per NIST guidelines. We deliver a comprehensive report with prioritized remediation steps, formatted for DoD compliance.
Benefit: Your networks become DoD-compliant fortresses, resilient against advanced threats and ready for CMMC certification.

Auditing & Compliance
NIST Alignment: Our audits align with NIST SP 800-171’s 97 controls and CSF 2.0’s Govern function to ensure compliance with CMMC, NIST 800-171, and FISMA. We use NIST’s machine-readable formats for transparent documentation.
[](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2024/05/nist-finalizes-updated-guidelines-protecting-sensitive-information) Process: We perform gap analyses, develop remediation plans, and provide ongoing monitoring to maintain compliance. Our reports support DoD audits and certification processes, ensuring no stop-work orders or penalties.
Benefit: Clients secure DoD contracts with confidence, knowing their systems meet federal standards and avoid costly non-compliance risks.

[](https://compliancy-group.com/dod-cybersecurity-dfars-and-nist-sp-800-171-compliance/)

Penetration Testing
NIST Alignment: Our tests simulate real-world attacks per CSF 2.0’s Detect and Respond functions, validating NIST 800-171 controls like incident response (03.06.01) and system integrity. We align with CMMC Level 3 to counter Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs).
[](https://www.nist.gov/publications)[](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/dod-cybersecurity-standards/) Process: Certified pentesters conduct tailored external and internal attacks, including social engineering, followed by detailed reports with NIST-compliant mitigation strategies.
Benefit: Like cyber war games sanctioned by NIST’s rulebook, our tests uncover vulnerabilities, ensuring your systems are battle-ready for DoD and commercial threats.
